Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about motor insurance in Nigeria

Is motor insurance mandatory in Nigeria?

Yes, third-party motor insurance is mandatory for all vehicles on Nigerian roads as stipulated by the Insurance Act. Driving without valid insurance is an offense punishable by law with fines up to ₦250,000 or imprisonment.

What's the difference between Comprehensive and Standalone?

Comprehensive includes both own-damage cover AND third-party liability protection—it's complete coverage. Standalone covers only damage to your own vehicle—it does NOT include third-party liability. Standalone is ideal if you already have third-party coverage elsewhere.

How much does car insurance cost in Nigeria?

Third-party insurance starts from ₦15,000 per year. Comprehensive insurance typically costs between 3% to 5% of the vehicle's value. Final premiums depend on vehicle type, age, engine capacity, usage, and location.

How quickly can I get my insurance certificate?

With VehicleInsure, you receive your digital insurance certificate immediately after payment. It's valid for all legal purposes. Physical documents can be delivered within 3-5 working days at no extra cost.

What documents do I need for motor insurance?

You'll need a valid means of identification (National ID, Driver's License, or International Passport), vehicle particulars (registration number, make, model, year), and proof of address. For comprehensive cover, vehicle inspection photos may be requested.

How do I file a claim?

You can file a claim online through your dashboard, via our mobile app, or by calling our 24/7 claims hotline at 0800-CLAIM-HELP. You'll need to provide accident details, photos, and a police report (for theft or major accidents).
