General Questions

Is motor insurance mandatory in Nigeria?

Yes, third-party motor insurance is mandatory for all vehicles on Nigerian roads as stipulated by the Insurance Act of 2003. Driving without valid insurance is an offense punishable by law with fines up to ₦250,000 or imprisonment. VehicleInsure makes it easy to get compliant coverage starting from just ₦5,000 per year.

How quickly can I get my insurance certificate?

With VehicleInsure, you receive your digital insurance certificate immediately after successful payment. The certificate is valid for all legal purposes including vehicle registration and FRSC checks. Physical documents can be delivered to your address within 3-5 working days at no extra cost.

What documents do I need to get insured?

For a quick and easy process, you'll need:

  • Valid means of identification (National ID, Driver's License, or International Passport)
  • Vehicle particulars (registration number, make, model, year)
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • For comprehensive cover, vehicle inspection photos may be requested
Is VehicleInsure licensed by NAICOM?

Yes! VehicleInsure is fully licensed and regulated by the National Insurance Commission (NAICOM), the apex regulatory body for insurance in Nigeria. Our license number is RIC-2020-089. We operate in partnership with leading underwriting firms to ensure your policy is backed by solid financial strength.

Coverage & Plans

What's the difference between Third-Party and Comprehensive insurance?

Third-Party Insurance: Covers damage to other people's property and injuries to third parties only. It does NOT cover damage to your own vehicle. This is the minimum legal requirement.

Comprehensive Insurance: Covers third-party liability PLUS damage to your own vehicle from accidents, theft, fire, flood, and vandalism. It provides complete protection for your investment.

Does comprehensive insurance cover flood damage?

Yes! Our comprehensive and premium plus policies include flood and natural disaster coverage. This is particularly important for Nigerian drivers, especially in flood-prone areas like Lagos, Port Harcourt, and parts of the Niger Delta. Claims for flood damage are processed quickly with proper documentation.

Is my car covered if someone else is driving it?

Yes, our comprehensive policies cover any authorized driver operating your vehicle with your permission. However, the driver must hold a valid Nigerian driver's license. For regular additional drivers (like a spouse or family member), we recommend adding them as named drivers on your policy for full coverage certainty.

Do you offer coverage for commercial vehicles?

Absolutely! We offer specialized coverage for commercial vehicles including taxis, Uber/Bolt vehicles, delivery vans, and trucks. Our commercial coverage includes passenger liability, goods-in-transit, and fleet discounts for multiple vehicles.

  • Standard claims: 3-5 business days
  • Major claims (theft/total loss): 7-14 business days

Delays typically only occur if documentation is incomplete or further investigation is required.

What should I do immediately after an accident?

Follow these steps for a smooth claims process:

  1. Ensure everyone is safe and seek medical attention if needed
  2. Do NOT admit fault or make agreements with third parties
  3. Take clear photos of the accident scene and all vehicle damage
  4. Exchange information with other involved parties
  5. Report to the nearest police station if required (especially for serious accidents)
  6. Call our 24/7 claims hotline or file online immediately
